Perspective/Viewpoint:

The first person subjective viewpoint is the best way to tell your personal story. That's because the story is about YOU, and only you can best explain your experience, how you felt, and illustrate in words the transformational results. First person narrative includes: descriptions of people/place/things, feelings/emotions, colors/sounds, dialogue, etc. Paint a picture to show the reader your feelings, your impressions, your pivotal moments (epiphanies, revelations, "aha!" moments, etc.), so they can see exactly what you saw, feel what you felt, and better understand your unique transformational journey!


Character: You are unique because of who you are, because of your particular "character," and you have survived whatever life has thrown at you! The question is WHY? Strength of character is key in your triumph! Describe what made you strong (even if you didn't think you were at the time it all happened!) Character is almost more important than the events of your story-what happened to you may not be unique, but the reader is learning how you dealt with it in your own, specific, unique way...


Context: The "BASIC FIVE's" of all writing: The Who, What, Where, How and When of your story. Be sure not to neglect them, for they present a context into which your readers "enter" your story. "When" and "where" are settings that allow the reader to place you in time and location. "What" explains the challenges/obstacles/goals you came into contact with that created your dilemma, problem, issue. "Who" represents any of the players with whom you came into contact during your transformational journey. And "Why" is the most important question of all! Why are you writing about your experience now? What did you learn, gain, modify or change?


Conflict: All good writing (both fiction and non-fiction) is based on drama. And drama cannot occur without CONFLICT. Identify and describe your major conflict: Was it internal or external? The three major themes in all of good literature include: Man vs. God; Man vs. Man; and Man vs. Nature. Which conflict was your transformational battle about? Did you battle your inner demons and/or your intuition, or did you challenge your best friend, boss, mother, doctor, significant other? Or is your story one of survival against the forces of nature-an accident, a loss, a natural disaster, etc?
Writing Guidelines:

  • Keep your sentences short--run-on sentences can be confusing! Try to limit them to a 40 word max as a rule of thumb! Limit paragraphs to 2-3 sentences.
  • Use "active" verbs: instead of "is," "am," "are," "was," "were," or other derivatives of the verb "to be," substitute verbs that show us action...
  • Employ "modifiers," i.e., descriptive terms...
  • Avoid the use of "passive" tense. Any "-ing" or "ly" can be made active
  • Use quotes when appropriate to liven up your copy


For example, replace "I was running down the street carrying my shoes in my hand, not even paying attention to where I was going so quickly." with "I raced down the crowded street, shoes clutched in one hand, oblivious to the noises of the city and the people who stopped to stare."
Structure: Include a beginning, middle and end. Simple as that! Start with a basic outline and fill it in-you only have a maximum of 1,500 words, so build your story carefully, making sure it flows naturally. Eliminate the unnecessary A very good exercise to help you hone in on your "real" story would be to try to journal. This is a common technique in writing personal stories, for it allows the free flow of information to hit the page without censorship. In other words, don't think, just write-what happened? How did it feel? What did others say to you or you to them? What did it all look like? Oftentimes your story will come alive when you write down your thoughts, feelings, and impressions without any goal in mind. Perhaps you thought your story was about one thing, but journaling helps you narrow it to a more specific topic. Let us in to see what that is and how it affected your transformation into the person you have become! Good writing involves revealing yourself: it is the act of revealing that allows your audience to "connect" with you! How can the reader connect if she cannot understand the emotions behind what happened? Your transformation was emotional, with a before, during and after-what were the specifics of yours?
Think of Your Story as Symbolic: Life as Myth: Yours is the "hero's journey"--you were plucked from ordinary life, forced to embark on a journey that involved transiting into the metaphorical underworld...there you faced the dragon, slayed him, and--triumphant!--gained the gift of enlightenment. You then brought that insight back to the world to share it with your "tribe." What are you "privileged" now to know? What is it that you have to share? Now that you are a "hero," what is the point of your transformational journey?
Get Started! Remember, an editor will help shape your article into the best it can be! It's your story that's important, so don't obsess about too many details. Make sure you run through this checklist and smooth out any rough edges.
Be as clear, concise, and as straightforward as possible so as not to confuse the reader. Get to the point in a simple, direct manner by using words that move and descriptions that resonate, and you will captivate your readers and leave them satisfied! NEW
